## Gemini Main Page
[Parent Page](Models)

Gemini is Google’s general-purpose AI reasoning engine used within the Google ecosystem.

It is built by **Google** and serves as:

- A reasoning assistant  
- A document and spreadsheet assistant  
- A research and summarization tool  
- A multimodal analysis tool (text, images, documents)  
- An embedded productivity accelerator inside Google Workspace  

In this program, Gemini is treated as a **productivity integration layer**, not an autonomous decision-maker.

---

## What Gemini Is

Gemini is a large language model integrated deeply into Google’s ecosystem (Docs, Sheets, Gmail, Drive).

It can:

- Analyze and generate text  
- Assist with document drafting  
- Summarize long documents and threads  
- Help structure spreadsheets  
- Assist with presentations  
- Interpret images and mixed-format documents  

It works best when used directly inside Google Workspace workflows.

---

## What Gemini Is Not

- It is **not always correct**.
- It does **not replace professional financial judgment**.
- It does **not automatically understand your business constraints**.
- It may generate confident but incorrect numerical logic.
- It does not verify real-time market data unless explicitly connected to trusted sources.

All outputs must be reviewed before operational use.

---

## Primary Use Cases in This Program

Within AI Training (Commercial Real Estate focus), Gemini is used for:

- Drafting memos and reports in Google Docs  
- Assisting with financial models in Google Sheets  
- Structuring deal summaries  
- Summarizing long email chains  
- Creating presentation outlines in Slides  
- Organizing research stored in Drive  

Gemini is strongest when embedded directly into document workflows.

---

## Strengths

- Native integration with Google Workspace  
- Multimodal capabilities (text + image + document context)  
- Strong summarization and document restructuring  
- Smooth collaboration in shared documents  
- Useful for teams already standardized on Google tools  

---

## Weaknesses

- Less flexible outside the Google ecosystem  
- May struggle with complex multi-step financial modeling  
- Can hallucinate references or assumptions  
- Limited customization compared to API-first systems  
- Context resets more easily than structured agent workflows  

Improper use can introduce subtle spreadsheet or document errors.

---

## Our Operating Method

We do not use Gemini as a passive autocomplete tool.

We use it through:

- Structured prompts inside Docs and Sheets  
- Clear statement of financial assumptions  
- Explicit request for logic breakdowns  
- Iterative refinement  
- Manual validation of calculations  

Gemini supports workflow efficiency — but discipline remains human.

---

## Governance Notes

- Do not expose confidential financial data without authorization.  
- Review all spreadsheet formulas generated by AI.  
- Confirm assumptions in financial projections.  
- Maintain human approval before distributing AI-generated reports.  

Enterprise trust depends on oversight.

---

## Position in the AI Stack

Gemini is our primary **Google Workspace integration model**.

Other models may be used for:

- Deep reasoning and coding (ChatGPT)  
- Long-context policy or document analysis (Claude)  
- Web-grounded research (Perplexity)  

Tool selection should be task-driven.

---

## Starting Recommendation

New users should begin by:

1. Using Gemini to summarize existing Docs.  
2. Asking it to explain spreadsheet logic step-by-step.  
3. Requesting structured outlines before drafting reports.  
4. Verifying all numerical outputs manually.  
5. Applying it to one recurring document workflow per week.

Gemini is most powerful when it improves existing workflows — not when it replaces thinking.
